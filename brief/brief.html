The brief in
one breath

We are building a family of developer tools under one house: ALKMY. The frontend framework is ALB'DO. Under it sit FORGE (backend), PHOSPHOR (the client runtime), CTRNI'TAS (AI), and RUB'DO (fintech). Your job is to give this house a face someone remembers after one glance and trusts after ten seconds.

The one line

Base matter, transmuted. Every product takes something raw — code, data, money, thought — and returns it refined, mirror-finished, permanent. That is literally what alchemy is, and it is exactly what these tools do.

Why it matters

Our competitors look like infrastructure: cold, technical, forgettable. We are going to look like a rare object under museum light — heavy, precious, a little uncanny. Developers should feel they are holding something crafted, not configured.

What this document is

Not a logo request. It is the thematic spine — the idea, the material, the palette, the typography, the rules — so that every artifact you make, and every one we make after you, belongs to the same world. Read it once end-to-end before you sketch.

North star. If a stranger saw a single frame of our work with no logo on it, they should still feel: dark, precious, transmuted, slightly alive. Chrome in a void, breathing under a rim of light.

The spine:
the magnum opus

Alchemy's magnum opus — the Great Work — is a sequence of stages that turn prima materia (raw, black, formless matter) into the lapis (the philosopher's stone: perfected, permanent, alive). The classical stages are colours. Our product names are those stages. This is not decoration bolted on afterward — it is the reason the names exist, and it gives every product a fixed place, a colour, and a role.

Nigredo → FORGE

The blackening

The prima materia — dissolution, the crucible, matter broken down to be remade. Our backend: the black vessel where raw input is transmuted. Everything is born from this black; ember heat inside the dark.

Albedo → ALB'DO

The whitening

Washing to silver. The mirror stage — purification, the moon, reflection. Our frontend: the face users see, rendered as polished chrome. It reflects.

Citrinitas → CTRNI'TAS

The yellowing

The solar dawn — the awakening of wisdom and light. Our AI: intelligence breaking over the work like gold at sunrise. Gold, glass, the lit brain.

Rubedo → RUB'DO

The reddening

The final work — the stone achieved, blood, life, value made real. Our fintech: the point where the whole process becomes worth something. Oxblood red.

The one that carries the light

The four coloured stages are the four products. One name isn't a colour-stage — it is the light that makes the whole opus visible:

  • PHOSPHOR — the light-bearer (and the glowing coat inside every screen). Our client runtime: the thing that makes the work glow. It is the rim-light itself — the bloom behind every object on the board.

And ALKMY is the alchemist — the house that holds all four stages and the light.

Why this is the right spine. It is not a theme we chose — it is the meaning already inside the names. FORGE, ALB'DO, CTRNI'TAS and RUB'DO are the four coloured stages of the opus, in order — Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, Rubedo. PHOSPHOR is the light that reveals them. The board's chrome anatomy is exactly what the opus promised: the mortal, made incorruptible. Everything agrees. We just have to draw it.

ALKMY / Brand Identity04 — The Signature Mark
The cut · distillation
04 / 10

The apostrophe
is the signature

ALB'DO. RUB'DO. CTRNI'TAS. The dropped vowel is permanent and intentional — a wordmark rule, not a typo. It has a meaning, and that meaning is the whole brand in one keystroke.

What it means

Alchemy's core move is the removal of the superfluous — distillation, boiling away the impure until only the essence remains. The apostrophe is the seam where base matter was cut away. The word is distilled: the vowel evaporates, the mark shows where it used to be.

What it rhymes with

  • The chromatic-aberration split — light cut into cyan/magenta seams
  • The tilde in the khẽr wordmark on the board
  • The gap between two reaching hands that never quite touch
  • A caesura — a held breath, a beat of silence in the name
Design direction — the glyph

Treat the apostrophe as the hero glyph of the whole system, not a stock punctuation mark. Options to explore:

  • A polished chrome sliver — a bead of mercury caught mid-fall
  • A prismatic split — the one place colour (cyan/magenta) enters an otherwise monochrome wordmark
  • A vertical seam of light — as if the word were cut and lit from inside
  • An alchemical tick — echoing the sigils on the etched can

It should be legible at 12px and iconic at 2 metres. It can stand alone as a favicon / app-icon seed.

"The mark is not missing a letter.
It is showing you what we removed."

ALKMY / Brand Identity05 — Material & Palette
Chrome · glass · void · bloom
05 / 10

Material
& palette

The four materials

  • Nigredo black — the ground. Near-total black, faint warmth. Everything floats in it.
  • Liquid chrome — the hero. Mirror-polished, banded reflections, always flowing, never matte.
  • Clear glass — the secondary. Refraction, thickness, prismatic edges. For "seeing through" — intelligence, clarity.
  • The bloom — light as material. A white rim-halo behind every object. This is Phosphor.

The one accent: aberration

Colour enters only as chromatic aberration — the cyan/magenta seam at the edge of refracted light. Used like salt. The base brand is monochrome; the spectrum is a rumour at the edges.

Per-stage tint (children only)

The house is silver. Each child may carry one stage-colour, used sparingly against chrome — never a full flood. This keeps the family unified and the products distinct.

ALKMY / Brand Identity06 — Typography & Composition
Grotesque + monospace
06 / 10

Typography &
composition

Two voices

Display — a heavy neo-grotesque. Swiss, tight, uppercase, confident. Think the weight of Helvetica Now Display / Neue Haas Grotesk / Archivo Black. It carries the wordmarks and the big statements. Set it tight — negative tracking, letters almost touching.

Marginalia — a monospace. Everything technical is set in mono: dates, versions, coordinates, captions, the "PROMT / spec" language, barcodes. It is the sound of a laboratory logbook. Small, wide-tracked, uppercase.

Composition: object in the void

  • One subject, centred, floating in black with a bloom behind it. Give it air — let the void do the work.
  • Spec-sheet scaffolding — hairline rules, corner ticks, rotated sidebar labels, tiny mono coordinates in the margins. The chaos of a technical document framing a precious object.
  • Barcodes, index numbers, "00 / 10" — treat every layout like a catalogue plate of a rare artifact.
  • High contrast, generous negative space. Never busy. The luxury is in the emptiness.

ALKMY / Brand Identity07 — The Anatomy Motif
The mortal, made permanent
07 / 10

The anatomy
motif

The board keeps returning to the body — heart, brain, ribcage, hands, a face — always cast in chrome or glass, never in flesh. This is the emotional core of the brand, and it is the truest thing alchemy ever said.

The Great Work was never really about making gold. It was about making the mortal incorruptible — taking what decays and rendering it permanent, luminous, whole. A heart that will never stop. A hand that stays reaching.

How to use it

  • Reserve anatomy for hero moments — launch art, covers, the emotional beat. Not UI chrome.
  • Always transmuted — chrome, glass, crystal. Never realistic, never gory, never medical.
  • Match organ to product where it's honest: heart → RUB'DO, brain → CTRNI'TAS, reaching hands → ALB'DO.
  • Keep it reverent. This motif carries the weight of the whole company. It is dignity, not shock.

Handle with care. This is the one place the brand is allowed to be tender. It should feel like something held, not something dissected — the wish that the things we love could be made to last. Draw it the way you'd draw a relic, not a diagram.

ALKMY / Brand Identity08 — The Myth & Voice
The view from above
08 / 10

The myth
& the voice

Phaethon · the view from above

The narrative undertone of the whole house is the myth of Phaethon (and his cousin Icarus) — the mortal who took the reins of the sun's chariot and flew too close. But we are not telling the story of the fall. We are telling the story of the view from above — the moment a person who was told "you can't" sees the whole world laid out beneath them, because they dared to fly the machine themselves.

That is what building on ALKMY should feel like: ordinary people handed the reins of the sun. The board's reaching hands, paper plane, and butterfly are that myth — ascent, flight, metamorphosis, the chariot of gold (Citrinitas).

Voice & tone

  • Precise, not loud. We speak like a lab logbook and a poet at once — technical nouns, restrained awe.
  • Confident, never boastful. The work looks expensive; the words don't need to shout it.
  • Reverent about craft. We talk about making, transmuting, refining — not "shipping."
  • A little uncanny. There is always something alive under the chrome. Let it be slightly strange.
  • Honest. No hype-cycle language. We earned the awe; we describe it plainly.
Words we live in

transmute · refine · distil · forge · mirror · bloom · incorruptible · prima materia · the Great Work · the view from above.

Words we avoid

disrupt · seamless · leverage · synergy · next-gen · game-changer · anything a hundred other startups already said.

ALKMY / Brand Identity09 — Guardrails
Do · Don't
09 / 10

Guardrails

If you remember nothing else, remember these. They keep every future artifact inside the same world.

Do

  • Start every frame from true black and let objects float in it
  • Render the hero in flowing chrome or clear glass, always with a rim-bloom
  • Keep it 90% monochrome; let a single stage-colour identify a child product
  • Set display type heavy, tight, uppercase; set all technical text in mono
  • Frame objects like catalogue plates — hairlines, ticks, coordinates, index numbers
  • Honour the apostrophe as the hero glyph in ALB'DO / RUB'DO / CTRNI'TAS
  • Let the work feel precious, permanent, and faintly alive

Don't

  • No gradients-for-fun, neon floods, or rainbow UI — colour is salt, not paint
  • No flat / matte / cartoon renders — if it isn't reflective, it isn't ours
  • No stocky "tech" clichés — circuit boards, glowing grids, robots, hexagons
  • No busy layouts — the luxury is the emptiness; don't fill the void
  • No gore or medical realism in the anatomy — transmuted and reverent only
  • Never drop or restyle the apostrophe as generic punctuation
  • Don't make it warm and friendly — it is cool, exacting, a little uncanny

The one-frame test. Cover the logo. If the frame still reads dark · precious · transmuted · slightly alive, it's ALKMY. If it reads generic SaaS, start over.
